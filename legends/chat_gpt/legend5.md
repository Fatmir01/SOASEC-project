# Title — Funding Equality by Design

## Setting

GreenForge Municipality is a regional public authority in
Cluj-Napoca, Romania, responsible for urban development, local employment
programs, and EU-funded infrastructure projects. The municipality manages
multiple funding streams, including cohesion policy funds and social innovation
grants.

## Characters

- **Andrei Popescu** — Director of EU Funds, overseeing project design and
  compliance with funding regulations
- **Daria Ionescu** — Gender Equality Officer, responsible for integrating
  gender mainstreaming into public programs
- **Mikkel Sørensen** — External Auditor, evaluating compliance with EU funding
  conditions
- **Sorina Vlad** — Head of Social Services, managing childcare and community
  support initiatives
- **Ibrahim Kaya** — Local NGO Coordinator, representing civil society partners
  in funded projects

---

## The Story

The turning point came when GreenForge Municipality applied for
a major urban development grant under the European Regional Development Fund.
The proposal included investments in business incubators and transport
infrastructure, but during the preliminary review, a critical issue emerged.

Mikkel Sørensen, the external auditor, flagged the application. "Your proposal
lacks a clear gender equality framework. Under the Common Provisions Regulation,
this is an enabling condition. Without it, the funding cannot be approved."

Andrei Popescu frowned. "We've included general inclusion language. Isn't that
sufficient?"

Daria Ionescu intervened. "It's not. The EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025
requires concrete integration of gender considerations. We need a structured,
measurable approach."

The municipality quickly realized that compliance was not just a formality—it
required systemic change. Daria led the development of a **Local Gender Equality
Strategic Framework**, aligned with EU principles and tailored to GreenForge's
socio-economic context.

"We need to connect funding to outcomes," Daria explained during a planning
session. "Every euro must contribute to reducing gender gaps."

The revised plan introduced several targeted measures. First, part of the
funding was reallocated to establish three new childcare centers in underserved
districts, addressing a key barrier to women's labour market participation.
Sorina Vlad emphasized the urgency: "We have waiting lists of over 200 families.
Without childcare, many women cannot work."

Second, the business incubator program was redesigned to include a dedicated
track for women entrepreneurs. This included mentorship, access to
micro-financing, and training in digital skills. Ibrahim Kaya's NGO was
contracted to deliver outreach programs, particularly targeting migrant and
rural women.

"We're finally reaching women who were invisible in previous funding cycles,"
Ibrahim noted.

Third, Andrei implemented a gender budgeting mechanism. All funded projects were
required to report gender-disaggregated data and demonstrate how expenditures
contributed to equality objectives. This aligned with the EU's push to measure
gender impact across the Multi-Annual Financial Framework.

Mikkel reviewed the updated proposal. "This is now compliant. You've
operationalized gender mainstreaming, not just described it."

Additionally, GreenForge secured complementary funding through the European
Social Fund Plus to support training programs for women re-entering the
workforce after care-related career breaks. A partnership with a local
university ensured monitoring and evaluation of outcomes.

Implementation was closely tracked. Within 18 months, the results were clear:
over 120 new childcare places were created, enabling a 17% increase in
employment among mothers in targeted districts. The women's entrepreneurship
track supported 45 new businesses, 60% of which were still operational after one
year.

During a public review, Andrei reflected, "We used to see funding as a financial
tool. Now we see it as a lever for structural change."

Daria added, "And we've aligned our entire funding strategy with the EU Gender
Equality Strategy 2020-2025."

GreenForge Municipality's approach was later shared as a best practice example,
demonstrating how embedding gender equality into funding design can produce
measurable, lasting impact.
