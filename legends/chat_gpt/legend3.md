# Title — Shattering the Turbine's Glass Ceiling

## Setting

FjordWind Energy is a prominent renewable energy consortium headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark. Operating within the highly competitive offshore wind sector, the company oversees complex, large-scale infrastructure projects and grid integrations across the North and Baltic Seas.

## Characters

- **Soren** (Chair of the Board): A seasoned industry veteran who recognizes the pressing need to modernize the cooperative's leadership pipeline and succession planning.
- **Aisha** (Director of Corporate Governance): She is tasked with aligning the company's internal policies with emerging EU directives on corporate diversity and inclusion.
- **Frederik** (Head of Executive Recruitment): He is responsible for sourcing and shortlisting candidates for C-suite and board-level vacancies.
- **Maja** (Senior Lead Engineer): A highly qualified technical expert who exemplifies the pool of untapped female talent currently stalled within the organization's middle management.

---

## The Story

During the annual review of FjordWind Energy's corporate governance report, Aisha identified a glaring structural inequality. While the company had successfully achieved gender parity among junior engineers and middle management, the upper echelons told a different story: the non-executive board and the C-suite were 90% male. This bottleneck effectively trapped high-performing women like Maja beneath a persistent glass ceiling.

Aisha called a strategic meeting with Soren and Frederik to address the data. "Our lower levels are incredibly diverse, but our top tier remains static," Aisha explained, projecting the organizational chart. "Because these top positions have been held almost exclusively by men for the past decade, our recruitment pattern for successors relies heavily on their existing, male-dominated professional networks. We are perpetuating an unconscious bias."

Frederik frowned, looking over the hiring data. "Our mandate has always been to hire the most qualified candidate. Are you suggesting we change our merit-based approach?"

"I am suggesting that our current definition of 'merit' is skewed," Aisha countered firmly. "If we want innovative approaches to navigate the complex challenges of the green energy transition, we need inclusive leadership. The EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025 explicitly emphasizes that a broad range of talents drives better corporate governance and economic growth. We need to proactively break this glass ceiling and aim for the 40% minimum target for non-executive board members from the under-represented sex."

Soren nodded in agreement. "She is right, Frederik. We cannot afford to let unconscious bias dictate our succession planning. Let's overhaul the recruitment pipeline immediately."

To bridge this concrete gap, the team implemented a multi-tiered corporate governance reform. First, Aisha and Frederik established a strict "Diverse Slate" policy, mandating that at least 40% of the candidates on any final shortlist for a board or executive vacancy must be female. Second, to cultivate internal talent, FjordWind launched the "Board Sponsorship Initiative." This required current executives to actively sponsor and advocate for high-performing female middle managers. Soren personally sponsored Maja, integrating her into high-level stakeholder negotiations and board-shadowing sessions. Finally, the company mandated rigorous unconscious bias training for all current board members involved in executive promotion decisions.

Within three years, the implementation of these targeted measures yielded highly measurable success. FjordWind Energy's non-executive board reached 44% female representation, completely transforming its decision-making dynamics. Furthermore, through the sponsorship initiative, Maja was successfully promoted to Chief Technology Officer, leading the design of a highly profitable new offshore turbine model. By systematically dismantling the barriers to top leadership, FjordWind Energy proved that diverse corporate governance directly enhances business performance, fully realizing the objectives of the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025.
