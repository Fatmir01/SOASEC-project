# Title — Blueprint for Discovery: Funding an Equitable Horizon

## Setting

Corrib BioSciences is an independent biomedical research institute based in
Galway, Ireland. Operating within the biotechnology and pharmaceutical sector,
the institute specializes in developing targeted immunotherapies and relies
heavily on competitive international funding to sustain its clinical trials and
laboratory operations.

## Characters

- **Dr. Siobhan** (Director of Research): She is responsible for securing major
  scientific grants and sustaining the institute's long-term operational budget.
- **Mateo** (Grants and Compliance Manager): He meticulously reviews all funding
  applications to ensure strict alignment with European Commission directives
  and financial prerequisites.
- **Dr. Anya** (Lead Immunologist): She designs the clinical trial protocols and
  methodologies for the institute's latest autoimmune treatments.

---

## The Story

During the final review of a multi-million-euro grant application for the
Horizon Europe program, Mateo flagged a critical deficiency in Corrib
BioSciences' submission. The institute was seeking funds to launch human trials
for a new rheumatoid arthritis therapy, but Mateo realized their institutional
framework was entirely non-compliant with the new European funding
prerequisites.

Mateo requested an immediate meeting with Dr. Siobhan and Dr. Anya. "We have a
major structural gap that will cost us this grant," Mateo stated, placing the
application on the table. "Our Horizon Europe submission will be rejected in the
administrative screening phase. The Commission has introduced strict new
measures to strengthen gender equality in research and innovation. We cannot
just ask for EU funding; we are now required to have a formal, publicly
available Gender Equality Plan in place as an applicant, which we currently do
not possess."

Dr. Siobhan sighed, leaning back in her chair. "We have always focused purely on
the science. I didn't realize our internal administrative policies would act as
a hard barrier to our clinical funding."

"It goes beyond our internal administration, Siobhan," Dr. Anya interjected,
looking critically at her own research protocols. "Mateo's warning made me
review our actual trial design. Autoimmune diseases disproportionately affect
women, yet our historical baseline data and our proposed clinical trials still
rely heavily on a standard 'male-default' physiological model. If the EU is
prioritizing funding for gender and intersectional research, our current
methodology is scientifically outdated."

"Exactly," Mateo affirmed. "The EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025 clearly
states that the integration of a gender dimension throughout the Multi-Annual
Financial Framework is mandatory. They want to ensure that resource allocation
actively contributes to equality objectives. If we want this Horizon Europe
funding, we must prove our commitment both institutionally and scientifically."

To address this concrete compliance gap, the team suspended the application and
implemented two specific, realistic measures. First, Siobhan and Mateo
collaborated with the human resources department to draft and publish a
comprehensive organizational Gender Equality Plan (GEP). This formal policy
instituted transparent, unbiased recruitment protocols for senior laboratory
positions, set concrete targets for female representation on the institute's
scientific advisory board, and established a dedicated budget to support
female-led technology spin-offs from their research.

Second, Dr. Anya overhauled the grant's scientific methodology. She redesigned
the clinical trial to explicitly align with the call for intersectional
research. The new protocol mandated that all pharmacological data be
disaggregated and analyzed by sex, age, and hormonal phases, ensuring the
resulting immunotherapy would be accurately dosed and effective for female
patients.

The implementation of these measures yielded a highly successful, measurable
outcome. Six months later, Corrib BioSciences not only secured the full €4.5
million Horizon Europe grant, but their application was specifically commended
by the review board as a model for inclusive scientific methodology. By aligning
their institutional framework and research design with the funding requirements
of the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025, the institute secured its
financial future while driving equitable medical innovation.
