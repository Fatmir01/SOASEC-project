# Title — "The Line We Do Not Cross"

## Setting

**SolariaTech**, a mid‑sized renewable‑energy engineering firm based in **Valencia, Spain**, has recently expanded its digital services division. As the company grows, it faces new challenges related to workplace culture, online collaboration, and ensuring that all employees feel safe and respected.

## Characters

- **Alex Rivas — HR Compliance Officer**  
  Oversees workplace safety and equality policies; initiates the investigation that drives the story.
- **Miren Solé — Senior Software Engineer**  
  Reports early signs of gender‑based harassment occurring in internal project channels.
- **Jonas Petrov — Team Lead, Digital Systems**  
  Initially hesitant to intervene due to project deadlines but becomes central to implementing solutions.
- **Samira El‑Kadi — External Trainer, Specialist in Violence Prevention**  
  Brought in to design targeted interventions aligned with EU standards.

---

## The Story

SolariaTech had just secured a major EU-funded contract to develop predictive maintenance software for offshore wind turbines. The digital division was under intense pressure: deadlines were tight, and the client expected a prototype within eight weeks. In the middle of this rush, **Miren Solé** approached **Alex Rivas** with a concern she had been hesitant to voice.

"There's a pattern in the project chat," she said quietly. "A few colleagues keep making comments about women being 'too sensitive' or 'not suited for field engineering.' Yesterday someone posted a meme mocking female technicians. It's subtle, but it's constant."

Alex felt the weight of the moment. The company could ignore the issue and keep the team focused on the prototype—or it could intervene and risk slowing down progress. But the choice was clear.  
**"The EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025 is explicit," Alex said. "We must ensure everyone is free from violence and stereotypes. This isn't optional."**

Alex brought the issue to **Jonas Petrov**, the team lead. Jonas frowned. "We're already behind schedule. If we start pulling people into workshops now, we'll lose days we don't have."  

Miren responded firmly: "If we don't address this, we're telling people like me that our safety and dignity are secondary to deadlines."

Jonas paused, then nodded. "You're right. Let's fix this properly."

Together, they designed a concrete plan:

1. **Immediate Action**  
   - Alex issued a formal reminder of SolariaTech's zero‑tolerance policy on harassment, referencing the **Istanbul Convention** and the company's obligations under EU law.  
   - The offending posts were documented, and individuals involved were privately informed that such behaviour constituted gender‑based harassment.

2. **Targeted Training**  
   - Jonas approved bringing in **Samira El‑Kadi**, an external specialist, to run a **two‑hour mandatory workshop** on preventing gender‑based violence, online harassment, and harmful stereotypes.  
   - The workshop included real case studies, role‑play scenarios, and clear reporting pathways.

3. **Structural Measures**  
   - Alex implemented a new **anonymous reporting tool** for digital misconduct.  
   - The team introduced **moderation guidelines** for all internal communication channels.  
   - A "Respect in Engineering" code of conduct was added to onboarding materials.

4. **Preventive Education**  
   - Samira conducted a follow‑up session focusing on masculinities, stereotype awareness, and non‑violent communication—directly reflecting the Strategy's emphasis on early prevention and cultural change.

Despite initial concerns, the project timeline recovered quickly. The team reorganised tasks to compensate for training hours, and productivity stabilised within a week.

By the end of the quarter, measurable improvements were evident:

- Reports of inappropriate comments in digital channels dropped by **82%**.  
- Employee survey results showed a **41% increase** in perceived psychological safety.  
- Female engineers' retention in the digital division rose from **78% to 91%**.  
- The EU project auditor commended SolariaTech for "exemplary alignment with the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025."

The experience became a turning point for the company. As Jonas later told Alex, "We didn't just meet a regulation—we built a better team."
