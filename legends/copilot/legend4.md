# Title — "The Grant That Changed the Tide"

## Setting

**MarisNova Cooperative**, a sustainable aquaculture and coastal‑innovation hub located in **Pula, Croatia**, works with local fishers, marine biologists and technology start‑ups. As the cooperative prepares to expand its research facilities, it seeks EU funding to strengthen gender equality in its workforce and community programmes.

## Characters

- **Teo Marin — Funding and Compliance Coordinator**  
  Leads the cooperative's applications for EU programmes and identifies gaps in gender‑equality readiness.
- **Jana Kovac — Marine Robotics Engineer**  
  One of the few women in the technical division; her experience highlights structural barriers.
- **Milan Stojan — Operations Director**  
  Focused on rapid expansion; initially reluctant to invest time in gender‑equality frameworks.
- **Dr. Amina Farouk — External Gender‑Mainstreaming Specialist**  
  Hired to help the cooperative meet EU enabling conditions and design gender‑responsive funding proposals.

---

## The Story

MarisNova Cooperative had ambitious plans: a new marine‑innovation lab, expanded training for local youth, and a pilot programme on sustainable aquaculture. To finance this growth, **Teo Marin** prepared applications for the **European Social Fund Plus (ESF+), the European Maritime and Fisheries Fund (EMFF)** and the **InvestEU Programme**.  

But as he reviewed the requirements, Teo noticed a major obstacle. "We don't have a national‑level gender equality strategic framework to reference," he told the leadership team. "And the **Common Provisions Regulation** requires this enabling condition before we can access funds for gender‑balance, childcare or labour‑market projects."

**Milan Stojan**, the Operations Director, frowned. "We're already stretched thin. Do we really need to overhaul our internal policies just to apply for funding?"

Before Teo could respond, **Jana Kovac** spoke up. "We have three women in the entire robotics division. Our training programmes don't reach women in coastal communities. And we've never analysed gender impacts in our projects. If we want EU support, we need to show we're serious."

Teo nodded. "The **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025** is clear: funding must drive progress. If we don't integrate gender equality into our planning, we won't qualify."

To move forward, Teo hired **Dr. Amina Farouk**, an expert in gender‑responsive budgeting. After conducting interviews and reviewing internal data, Amina presented her findings:

1. **Women were underrepresented in technical roles (only 14%).**  
2. **No gender‑impact assessments** had been conducted for training or community programmes.  
3. **No structured support** existed for women entrepreneurs in the cooperative's innovation incubator.  
4. **No monitoring system** tracked gender‑related outcomes.

"We need a comprehensive gender‑equality action plan," Amina said. "Not just for compliance, but to unlock the full potential of EU funding."

### Concrete Measures Implemented

**1. Creation of a Gender Equality Strategic Framework**  
Teo and Amina drafted a framework aligned with EU enabling conditions, including:  
- gender‑impact assessments for all new projects,  
- commitments to gender‑balanced recruitment panels,  
- targets for women's participation in training programmes.

**2. Launch of the "Blue Women Innovate" Programme**  
Funded through the EMFF call for women in the blue economy, the programme offered:  
- mentorship for women entrepreneurs,  
- seed grants for women‑led marine‑tech start‑ups,  
- partnerships with coastal women's associations.

**3. Gender‑Responsive Budgeting**  
Amina trained staff to track gender‑related expenditure across all EU‑funded projects, preparing the cooperative for future audits on gender mainstreaming in the EU budget.

**4. Inclusive Training and Outreach**  
Jana led a redesign of the robotics training curriculum, adding flexible schedules and community‑based workshops to reach women in rural coastal areas.

During a team meeting, Milan finally conceded: "I didn't realise how much funding we were leaving on the table. This isn't bureaucracy—it's strategy."

### Measurable Outcome

Within one year:

- MarisNova secured **€3.4 million** across ESF+, EMFF and InvestEU programmes.  
- Women's participation in technical training increased from **9% to 38%**.  
- The cooperative incubated **six women‑led start‑ups**, compared to zero the previous year.  
- Gender‑related expenditure tracking achieved **full compliance** during an external audit.  
- Staff surveys showed a **44% increase** in awareness of gender‑equality objectives.

MarisNova's annual report proudly stated that its progress "reflects the ambitions of the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025**, demonstrating how EU funding can drive real, measurable change."

The tide had turned—not just for the cooperative, but for the women shaping the future of the blue economy.
