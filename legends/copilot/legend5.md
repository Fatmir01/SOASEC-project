# Title — "The Framework That Opened the Door"

## Setting

**TerraVerde Rural Development Agency**, a public‑sector body based in **Alentejo, Portugal**, manages regional projects on sustainable agriculture, rural entrepreneurship and community services. As the agency prepares its 2021–2027 investment portfolio, it seeks to align with EU funding instruments that require strong gender‑equality integration.

## Characters

- **Marin Lopes — EU Programmes Officer**  
  Oversees applications to EU funds and identifies gaps in the agency's gender‑equality readiness.  
- **Elin Duarte — Rural Entrepreneurship Coordinator**  
  Works directly with women farmers and entrepreneurs; highlights barriers in access to finance.  
- **Soren Almeida — Financial Director**  
  Concerned about administrative workload and initially hesitant to adopt new enabling‑condition requirements.  
- **Dr. Kira Holm — External Gender‑Budgeting Expert**  
  Brought in to help the agency meet EU gender‑mainstreaming standards and design compliant funding proposals.

---

## The Story

TerraVerde Rural Development Agency was preparing a major funding package for the 2021–2027 cycle. The plan included upgrading childcare facilities in remote villages, supporting women‑led agricultural cooperatives, and launching a digital‑skills programme for rural youth.  

As **Marin Lopes** reviewed the requirements for the **European Social Fund Plus (ESF+), the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF)** and the **InvestEU Programme**, he realised something critical. "We can't access any of these funds unless we meet the enabling conditions," he told the leadership team. "We need a **national gender equality strategic framework** to reference, and we must demonstrate compliance with the **Charter of Fundamental Rights**, including gender equality."

**Soren Almeida**, the Financial Director, sighed. "We're already overloaded. Creating new frameworks and gender‑impact assessments will slow us down. Can't we just submit the applications as they are?"

Before Marin could respond, **Elin Duarte** spoke up. "Women in our region struggle to access finance, childcare and training. If we don't integrate gender equality properly, we're not just losing funding—we're failing the people we serve."

Marin nodded. "The **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025** makes it clear: funding must drive progress. If we want to invest in care facilities, women's entrepreneurship or training, we need to show real gender‑mainstreaming."

To move forward, Marin hired **Dr. Kira Holm**, a specialist in gender‑responsive budgeting. After reviewing TerraVerde's programmes, Kira presented her findings:

1. **No gender‑impact assessments** had been conducted for rural development projects.  
2. **Women's entrepreneurship support** was fragmented and underfunded.  
3. **Childcare infrastructure planning** lacked gender‑disaggregated data.  
4. **No monitoring system** tracked gender‑related expenditure or outcomes.

"We need a comprehensive gender‑equality action plan," Kira said. "Not just for compliance, but to unlock the full potential of EU funding."

### Concrete Measures Implemented

**1. Creation of a Gender Equality Strategic Framework**  
Marin and Kira drafted a framework aligned with the Common Provisions Regulation, including:  
- mandatory gender‑impact assessments for all new projects,  
- gender‑balanced steering committees,  
- measurable targets for women's participation in entrepreneurship and training.

**2. Launch of the "Women Grow Rural Futures" Initiative**  
Using ERDF and ESF+ funding, Elin designed a programme offering:  
- micro‑grants for women‑led agricultural start‑ups,  
- business‑planning workshops,  
- partnerships with local banks to reduce collateral barriers.

**3. Childcare Infrastructure Investment Plan**  
With Cohesion Fund support, TerraVerde mapped childcare gaps and funded:  
- three new rural childcare centres,  
- extended‑hours services for seasonal agricultural workers,  
- mobile early‑education units for remote villages.

**4. Gender‑Responsive Budgeting and Monitoring**  
Kira trained staff to track gender‑related expenditure across all EU‑funded projects. A new dashboard monitored:  
- gender distribution in programme participation,  
- funding allocated to gender‑equality objectives,  
- outcomes for women in rural labour markets.

During a review meeting, Soren admitted, "I didn't realise how much funding we were missing. This isn't extra work—it's smart investment."

### Measurable Outcome

Within eighteen months:

- TerraVerde secured **€5.1 million** across ESF+, ERDF, the Cohesion Fund and InvestEU.  
- Women's participation in entrepreneurship programmes rose from **22% to 57%**.  
- Childcare availability in the region increased by **34%**, enabling more women to take full‑time roles.  
- Gender‑related expenditure tracking achieved **full compliance** during an external audit.  
- Rural women's employment increased by **19%**, the highest rise in a decade.

In its annual report, TerraVerde wrote that its progress "demonstrates how EU funding—when aligned with the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025**—can transform rural communities and create lasting, measurable change."

The agency had not only unlocked funding—it had unlocked opportunity.
