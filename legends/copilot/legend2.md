# Title — "Balancing the Scales at NorthBridge Finance"

## Setting

**NorthBridge Finance Cooperative**, a community‑focused financial services provider in **Cork, Ireland**, has been struggling with staff shortages and uneven participation of women in full‑time roles. As the cooperative expands its digital lending services, it faces increasing pressure to meet client demand while ensuring fair, gender‑equal access to stable employment.

## Characters

- **Riley O'Shea — HR Development Manager**  
  Leads workforce planning and identifies structural barriers affecting women's participation.
- **Tomasz Novak — Senior Financial Analyst**  
  Works closely with teams experiencing staffing gaps; initially skeptical about flexible‑work reforms.
- **Eira Donnelly — Customer Services Associate**  
  A part‑time employee whose caring responsibilities highlight the cooperative's labour‑market gaps.
- **Lina Duarte — External Work‑Life Balance Consultant**  
  Brought in to design reforms aligned with EU directives and best practices.

---

## The Story

NorthBridge Finance Cooperative had grown rapidly over the past two years, especially after launching its digital micro‑lending platform. But with growth came strain: customer response times were slipping, and the board demanded a 15% improvement in service efficiency within the next quarter.  

During a routine workforce review, **Riley O'Shea** noticed a troubling pattern. "Seventy‑two percent of our part‑time staff are women," Riley told the management team. "Most of them cite caring responsibilities as the reason they can't take full‑time roles. This is a clear gender‑equality gap."

**Eira Donnelly**, who worked part‑time while caring for her elderly mother, confirmed the issue. "I'd love to take on more hours," she said, "but the current schedule is rigid. If I had even a bit of flexibility, I could contribute more."

The cooperative's leadership hesitated. **Tomasz Novak** voiced the concern many felt: "We're already behind on service targets. Introducing flexible arrangements now could slow us down even more."

Riley responded firmly. "The EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025 emphasises closing gender gaps in the labour market. And the **Work‑Life Balance Directive (EU) 2019/1158** requires us to support flexible working arrangements. This isn't just compliance—it's smart workforce planning."

To move forward, Riley brought in **Lina Duarte**, a consultant specialising in gender‑equal labour‑market participation. Together, they designed a targeted intervention:

### 1. Flexible Scheduling Pilot

Lina proposed a **three‑month pilot** allowing staff with caring responsibilities—regardless of gender—to request:
- adjustable start and end times,  
- compressed workweeks,  
- hybrid work arrangements for administrative tasks.

Eira was among the first to apply. "If this works," she said during a team meeting, "I can increase my hours by at least 25%."

### 2. Care‑Support Measures

Riley introduced:
- a **monthly care‑support stipend**,  
- partnerships with two local childcare providers offering discounted rates,  
- a resource hub listing community elder‑care services.

### 3. Gender‑Impact Monitoring

To ensure measurable progress, Tomasz helped develop a dashboard tracking:
- full‑time vs. part‑time participation by gender,  
- uptake of flexible arrangements,  
- productivity indicators across teams.

### 4. Awareness and Training

Lina delivered a workshop titled *"Thriving in a Gender‑Equal Economy"*, explaining how structural barriers—not individual choices—shape labour‑market participation. During the session, Tomasz admitted, "I didn't realise how much our old scheduling model limited people. This gives us a chance to rethink things."

### Measurable Outcome

By the end of the pilot:

- **41% of part‑time women increased their working hours**, compared to 8% before the pilot.  
- Customer response times improved by **17%**, surpassing the board's target.  
- Uptake of flexible arrangements was **gender‑balanced**, with 46% of applicants being men.  
- Staff satisfaction scores rose by **29%**, particularly regarding work‑life balance.  

NorthBridge Finance formally adopted the new model, noting in its annual report that the reforms "directly support the goals of the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025**, particularly in closing gender gaps in the labour market."

The cooperative had not only met its efficiency goals—it had built a more equitable workplace where everyone could thrive.
