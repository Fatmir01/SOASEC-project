# **Funding Equality Forward**

## **Setting**

A regional development agency in Bratislava, responsible for managing and allocating EU structural funds, including the European Social Fund Plus and the European Regional Development Fund. The agency coordinates with national ministries, municipalities, and civil society organizations to implement projects supporting economic development and social inclusion.

## **Characters**

* **Katarína Holubová** – Programme director overseeing EU fund allocation and compliance
* **Andrei Popescu** – Financial analyst responsible for evaluating project proposals
* **Zuzana Kováčiková** – Civil society coordinator liaising with NGOs and grassroots organizations
* **Leila Hassan** – Policy advisor specializing in gender equality and inclusion frameworks

## **The Story**

The challenge became evident during a mid-term review of funded projects under the current Multi-Annual Financial Framework. Katarína Holubová noticed a recurring issue: although significant resources were allocated to employment and infrastructure projects, very few explicitly addressed gender equality objectives.

“We are funding growth,” Katarína said during a strategy meeting, “but we’re not systematically funding equality. That’s a compliance gap.”

Leila Hassan agreed. “Under the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025, we are expected to integrate a gender dimension across all funding instruments—not treat it as optional.”

Andrei added a financial perspective. “Most proposals don’t include gender-disaggregated indicators. We can’t measure impact if it’s not built into the design.”

Zuzana, who worked closely with NGOs, pointed out another issue. “Smaller organizations, especially those working with vulnerable women, often lack the capacity to apply for EU funding. They’re effectively excluded.”

The team decided to overhaul their funding framework to ensure full alignment with EU requirements and unlock the potential of gender-responsive investment.

First, Katarína introduced a **mandatory gender equality criterion** in all funding calls. Every project proposal had to include a gender impact assessment, gender-disaggregated targets, and a description of how it would contribute to reducing inequalities. Proposals that failed to meet these criteria were deemed ineligible.

Second, Leila developed a standardized **Gender Equality Plan template** for applicants, particularly for those applying under research and innovation streams aligned with Horizon Europe. This template guided organizations in setting measurable objectives, such as increasing women’s participation in training programs or leadership roles.

Third, Andrei implemented a **gender budgeting tracking system**. Each funded project was assigned a “gender relevance score,” allowing the agency to quantify how much of its total budget contributed to gender equality objectives. Quarterly reports were generated to monitor compliance and inform future allocations.

To address access barriers, Zuzana launched a **capacity-building initiative** for civil society organizations. This included workshops on EU funding applications, one-on-one mentoring, and simplified application procedures for smaller grants under the Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values Programme. Special outreach was conducted to organizations supporting migrant women and women in rural areas.

During one workshop, a participant from a rural NGO remarked, “This is the first time we feel the system is designed for us to succeed.”

The agency also aligned its procedures with the Common Provisions Regulation by verifying that all funded projects operated within a national gender equality strategic framework and respected the Charter of Fundamental Rights.

Within a year, the impact was measurable. The proportion of funded projects with explicit gender equality objectives rose from 12% to 68%. Over €45 million was allocated to initiatives supporting women’s employment, entrepreneurship, and access to services. Participation of women-led organizations in funding programs increased by 50%.

At the annual review, Katarína concluded, “We’ve moved from passive compliance to active investment in equality.”

Leila added, “This is what gender mainstreaming in funding looks like—measurable, inclusive, and structural.”

The agency’s model was recognized at the national level as a benchmark for integrating gender equality into EU funding mechanisms, fully aligned with the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025**.
