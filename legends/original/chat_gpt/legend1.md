# **Breaking the Silence Protocol**

## **Setting**

A regional public hospital network operating in Gdańsk, specializing in emergency care and community health services. The institution employs over 1,200 staff and serves a diverse urban and rural population, with a growing focus on patient-centered care and compliance with EU public health standards.

## **Characters**

* **Avery Nowak** – Clinical governance officer responsible for policy compliance and quality standards
* **Marta Zielińska** – Senior nurse who frequently interacts with vulnerable patients in emergency units
* **Jonas Petraitis** – IT systems manager overseeing digital patient reporting tools
* **Elif Demir** – Psychologist coordinating trauma and victim support services

## **The Story**

The issue first surfaced during a routine internal audit led by Avery Nowak. While reviewing anonymized patient records, Avery noticed a troubling inconsistency: cases with clear indicators of domestic or gender-based violence were rarely flagged as such in the system. Despite EU directives and national guidelines, reporting remained sporadic and fragmented.

Avery called a meeting with Marta, Jonas, and Elif to understand the gap. “We’re seeing patients with injuries consistent with abuse,” Avery began, “but less than 10% are formally recorded as gender-based violence cases. That means they’re not receiving structured support.”

Marta nodded. “In the emergency room, we’re under pressure. Sometimes it’s hard to ask sensitive questions, especially without proper training. And honestly, some staff fear saying the wrong thing.”

Elif added, “And even when cases are identified, referrals to psychological support are inconsistent. Victims often leave without knowing their rights or available services.”

Recognizing that the problem required systemic intervention, the group designed a multi-layered response aligned with the principles of the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025.

First, Avery introduced a mandatory training program for all frontline staff, focusing on early identification of gender-based violence, trauma-informed communication, and legal obligations under the Victims’ Rights Directive. The training included role-playing scenarios and was co-developed with local NGOs specializing in domestic violence.

Second, Jonas upgraded the hospital’s digital reporting system. He integrated a confidential “GBV alert” feature into patient records, allowing staff to flag suspected cases with a single click. The system automatically triggered a protocol: notifying the on-call psychologist and generating a checklist for follow-up actions, including documentation, referral, and patient consent.

Third, Elif established a dedicated “Safe Pathway” for victims. This included a private consultation room, immediate psychological assessment, and a standardized information packet outlining legal rights, shelters, and support hotlines. The pathway ensured that no identified victim left the hospital without being offered structured support.

To reinforce accountability, Avery implemented monthly audits and published anonymized statistics internally. Departments were encouraged to share best practices, and staff could provide feedback anonymously.

Three months into implementation, the results were measurable. Reported cases of gender-based violence increased from 9% to 37% of suspected incidents. Referral rates to psychological services rose by 60%, and patient feedback indicated a significant improvement in perceived safety and support.

During a follow-up meeting, Marta reflected, “At first, it felt like extra work. But now, it’s part of how we care. We’re not just treating injuries—we’re addressing the cause.”

Avery concluded, “This is exactly what the EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025 calls for: effective prevention, victim-centered support, and coordinated action. We’ve turned a blind spot into a standard.”

The hospital network formally documented the protocol as a best practice model, contributing to national efforts aligned with the broader EU framework to prevent and combat gender-based violence.
