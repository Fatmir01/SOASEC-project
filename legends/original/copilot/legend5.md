# **Investing in Equality**

## **Setting**
This legend takes place at **Adriatic Innovation Hub**, a public–private research and training centre located in **Split, Croatia**. The Hub supports regional development projects in blue‑economy sectors, digital innovation and social inclusion, working closely with municipalities, NGOs and EU funding programmes.

## **Characters**
- **Mira Radić** — Director of EU Programmes; oversees funding applications and compliance with EU enabling conditions.  
- **Leonard Vuković** — Blue‑Economy Project Lead; develops training and entrepreneurship initiatives in maritime sectors.  
- **Hana Suleiman** — Social Inclusion Coordinator; works with migrant and asylum‑seeking women.  
- **Petra Novak** — Financial Analyst; monitors gender‑related expenditure across EU‑funded projects.  
- **Elena Rossi** — Young Marine Engineer; aspiring entrepreneur seeking support to launch a women‑led coastal‑technology start‑up.

## **The Story**
When Elena approached the Adriatic Innovation Hub with her idea for a women‑led coastal‑monitoring technology start‑up, Mira immediately recognised an opportunity. “This aligns perfectly with the EU’s funding priorities,” she told the team. “The **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020–2025** emphasises using the Multi‑Annual Financial Framework to advance gender equality. Let’s make this a flagship example.”

But before applying for support under the **European Maritime and Fisheries Fund (EMFF)** call dedicated to women in the blue economy, Mira needed to verify whether Croatia’s national authorities had fulfilled the **enabling conditions** required by the Common Provisions Regulation. After a review, Petra confirmed: “The national gender‑equality strategic framework is in place, and the Charter of Fundamental Rights enabling condition is fulfilled. We’re eligible.”

Leonard proposed expanding the project beyond entrepreneurship. “Why don’t we integrate training for women and girls in coastal engineering? It would address gender segregation in this sector and strengthen Elena’s future workforce.”

Meanwhile, Hana highlighted another funding opportunity. “The **Asylum and Migration Fund** encourages Member States to support migrant women’s integration. We could create a parallel programme offering technical apprenticeships for women with asylum backgrounds. It would strengthen both social inclusion and labour‑market participation.”

The team designed a comprehensive, multi‑fund initiative:

- An **EMFF‑funded accelerator** supporting women‑led blue‑economy start‑ups, including Elena’s.  
- A **European Social Fund Plus (ESF+) training pathway** for women entering marine engineering, with childcare support to improve work‑life balance.  
- A **Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values (CERV)** project to combat gender‑based violence in coastal communities and support vulnerable women in maritime workplaces.  
- A **Horizon Europe** research component requiring a gender‑equality plan and funding intersectional research on women’s participation in marine technology.  
- A **Common Agricultural Policy (CAP)** rural‑services grant to improve transport links for women in remote coastal villages attending training.  
- A **socially responsible public‑procurement clause** ensuring gender‑equality criteria in all Hub‑managed tenders.

During a project‑design workshop, Mira addressed the team. “Funding isn’t just about resources. It’s about shaping systems. If we integrate gender equality into every financial instrument, we can transform entire sectors.”

Petra developed a new internal tool to **track gender‑related expenditure**, anticipating the Commission’s push to measure gender‑equality spending in the 2021–2027 MFF. “This will help us demonstrate impact,” she explained. “And it prepares us for the findings of the European Court of Auditors’ audit on gender mainstreaming in the EU budget.”

Hana coordinated with local NGOs to ensure migrant women could access apprenticeships safely, including trauma‑informed support for those who had experienced gender‑based violence. Leonard partnered with universities to ensure the training modules aligned with Erasmus+ Inclusion and Diversity guidance.

Six months later, the results were impressive:  
- Elena’s start‑up secured EMFF funding and hired its first three employees — all women.  
- Thirty‑two women completed ESF+‑funded marine‑engineering training, with **40%** coming from rural areas.  
- Twelve migrant women began apprenticeships through the Asylum and Migration Fund component.  
- The Hub’s procurement reform increased the number of gender‑responsive tenders by **60%**.  
- Petra’s expenditure‑tracking tool was adopted by two neighbouring regions.

At the annual regional forum, Elena spoke to policymakers and community leaders. “This project didn’t just fund my idea,” she said. “It funded a pathway for women — Croatian, migrant, rural, young — to shape the future of the blue economy.”

The Adriatic Innovation Hub concluded that its integrated, multi‑fund approach—leveraging EU instruments to support entrepreneurship, inclusion, research and systemic change—represented concrete, measurable alignment with the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020‑2025**, demonstrating how funding can drive real progress toward a gender‑equal Europe.
