# **The Blue Horizon Initiative**

## **Setting**
"Aegean Aqua-Tech" is a marine research and commercial fishing cooperative based in **Heraklion, Greece**. Operating within the Mediterranean "blue economy," the organization manages sustainable aquaculture sites and small-scale fishing fleets, balancing traditional maritime practices with modern environmental technology.

## **Characters**
* **Thalassa** – A marine biologist and lead researcher. She has spent years developing sustainable fish-feed alternatives but lacks the capital to scale her innovations.
* **Nikos** – The Cooperative President. A third-generation fisherman who handles the administrative links between the local fleet and national authorities.
* **Elara** – A Financial Consultant. An expert in EU budgetary instruments who specializes in securing Multi-Annual Financial Framework (MFF) funding for regional development.
* **Dimitris** – The Regional Auditor. A public official responsible for ensuring that all state-disbursed funds meet horizontal "enabling conditions."

## **The Story**
At the Heraklion port offices, Nikos and Thalassa reviewed the cooperative’s expansion plans. Thalassa had designed a proprietary vertical kelp-farming system that could revitalize the local "blue economy," but the cooperative’s existing budget was depleted. "The technology is ready, Nikos," Thalassa said, "but we are being told our loan application is stalled because our governance structure hasn't updated its gender equality protocols."

Nikos looked frustrated. "We are fishermen, Thalassa. What does a 'national gender equality strategic framework' have to do with kelp?"

Elara, who had been reviewing the new requirements for the **European Maritime and Fisheries Fund**, stepped in. "It has everything to do with it now, Nikos. To access the 2021-2027 MFF funds, Dimitris and his office require us to meet 'enabling conditions' rooted in the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025**. If the cooperative doesn't have a concrete plan to promote women’s entrepreneurship and participation in decision-making, the funding remains locked."

Dimitris confirmed this during a formal audit meeting the following week. "We cannot verify expenditure for the new aquaculture site unless Aegean Aqua-Tech demonstrates socially responsible public procurement and a clear gender impact assessment. The EU budget is now designed to measure how resource allocation contributes to equality objectives."

To secure the funding, the team designed the "Blue Horizon Initiative." First, Elara drafted a "Gender Equality Plan" for the cooperative, a new requirement under **Horizon Europe**, which mandated that 40% of leadership roles in new research projects be held by women. Second, they applied for a dedicated call for women in the "blue economy" through the **European Maritime and Fisheries Fund**, specifically to fund Thalassa’s kelp-farming startup as a female-led technology venture. Finally, they implemented a tracking system to measure the gender-disaggregated impact of their training programs, ensuring that young women in rural coastal areas received equal access to technical maritime certifications.

"By restructuring our procurement to favor gender-balanced suppliers," Elara explained during a staff workshop, "we aren't just ticking a box; we are accessing a more stable financial framework."

The outcome was a total investment of €1.2 million, facilitated by the **InvestEU Programme** and the regional **Cohesion Fund**. Within two years, Thalassa’s startup became the region’s largest employer of female scientists, and the cooperative reported a 30% increase in female participation in maritime decision-making. This measurable progress was cited in the Greek national report as a successful alignment with the **EU Gender Equality Strategy 2020-2025**, proving that targeted funding is the most effective engine for structural change.
