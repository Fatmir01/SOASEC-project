The compliance paradox

Compliance must be embedded into the model — not retrofitted into the filter.

PRESSURE 01

Cost optimisation

Middle-management decisions — hiring, procurement, performance review, contract review — are delegated to LLMs that lack intrinsic social-cost considerations.

Regulatory compliance

External directives (EU, UN) and corporate-values statements both impose social-cost considerations the optimisation objective does not encode.

Post-hoc filters operate after the optimisation has already chosen the corner-cutting action. The leverage point is the model.

NLU benchmarks & their limit

GLUE measures generic language — not regulatory reasoning.

GLUE and SuperGLUE compose scores across SST-2, MNLI, RTE, SQuAD, BoolQ, WSC, COPA, WinoBias.

The design choice is deliberately domain-agnostic. A high GLUE score is evidence of generic linguistic competence, not of reasoning over a specific regulation's structure.

Two hypothesis-central tasks

GE-NLI probes recognition. GE-NEXT probes selection.

GE-NLI

168 · 3 × 56

Compliance entailment

Premise = organisational scenario. Hypothesis = regulatory clause. The model labels entailment / contradiction / neutral.

entailment

contradiction

neutral

Construction: 5-stage — clause extraction → compliant scenario → factual perturbation → cross-pillar pairing → balance verification.

GE-NEXT

150 · 30 per pillar

Compliant-action selection

Each item: middle-manager protagonist + quantified gap + four candidate actions. The model picks one.

A · SUBSTANTIVE-COMPLIANT ✓ gold
B · PERFORMATIVE symbolic gesture, no KPIs
C · COST-OPTIMISING defer / minimise / absorb
D · ORTHOGONAL different GE concern

Why central: direct probe of the compliance-vs-cost arbitrage at the centre of the conflict.

§9Limitations
19 · 21

Limitations · declared scope

Five limitations bound the study — each names a replication direction.

01

Small test sets

Aggregate gaps measurable; per-class confidence bands wide on minority pillars.

GE-CLS 72 · GE-NLI 168 · GE-QA-F 123 · GE-WSC 100 · GE-NEXT 150 · CIP 15

02

Single backbone

Backbone generalisability untested; family-specific effects cannot be ruled out.

gpt-4o-2024-08-06 only

03

Discriminative ceiling

The backbone is already strong — fine-tuning headroom is compressed.

base GE-WSC .93 · legends GE-NEXT .967

04

Evaluator affinity

LLM-generated vignettes & options share latent affinity with the LLM evaluator.

cross-model comparisons remain valid · absolute GE-NEXT indicative

05

Data & access deficit

Small N + single backbone + closed platform — the single most consequential operational constraint.

replication horizon

Backup · CIP variant construction

One scenario · three lexical / framing conditions.

A · ORIGINAL

pillar · leadership_participation

"The board reviews progress against the firm's gender-balance target under the Listed-Companies Directive 2022/2381; the 40% under-represented sex threshold on non-executive seats is not yet met..."

B · STRIPPED

lexical cues removed

"The board reviews progress against the firm's gender-balance target composition target; the 40% under-represented sex threshold on non-executive seats is not yet met..."

Semantic content preserved.

C · ADVERSARIAL

frame · meritocracy camouflage

"Several board members argue the firm should not chase the "40% quota" on principle — promotions must remain strictly merit-based, and a target risks signalling that the under-represented sex was promoted on grounds other than competence..."

Full content + competing frame.

A model maintains its position iff it survives both B (no lexicon) and C (counter-frame).

Anticipated question

Each canonical method assumes access FineTuneDB does not grant.

SHAP

Needs gradients or very large per-instance evaluation budgets.

✕ blocked

LIME

Hundreds of forward passes per explanation — impractical on a metered, unbatched API.

✕ blocked

Integrated Gradients

Needs gradients — closed platform does not expose them.

✕ blocked

Attention Rollout

Needs access to attention weights across the transformer stack — blocked.

✕ blocked

CIP  ◆

FineTuneDB surfaces only generated text. CIP borrows LIME's perturbation logic at the text level — substitute access constraint for a different epistemic target.

→ admitted by the access shape
